Getting started

What is Grrab

Grrab is a Chrome extension that grabs any image on the web in one shortcut.

Hover any image, press Cmd+C, and Grrab copies it to your clipboard — AI-upscaled to the resolution you can actually use, and saved to your collection in the background.

Install and start clipping in 30 seconds

  1. Add Grrab from the Chrome Web Store, then pin the Grrab icon to your toolbar.
  2. Open Pinterest, Google Images, or any page with an image. Hover over an image and press Cmd+C (Ctrl+C on Windows/Linux).
  3. Paste anywhere — Figma, a doc, an email. That’s your first clip.

Click the Grrab icon anytime to toggle Grrab on/off or adjust your shortcut and clip size.

Clipping to the clipboard works right away. To save clips to a collection, sign in with Google — covered in Your clip history.

How Grrab works

The clip shortcut

Hover any image on any page. Grrab highlights it and shows a "Press ⌘C to clip" tooltip.

Press the shortcut — Cmd+C on Mac, Ctrl+C on Windows and Linux. Grrab copies that exact image to your clipboard.

Grrab also works on video frames. Hover a playing video and press the shortcut to capture the current frame as an image.

To change the shortcut key: click the Grrab icon, then click the field under “Clip Shortcut” and press the key combination you want to use.

AI upscaling on your device

Reference images on the web are often too small to use. Grrab fixes this at the moment you clip.

Click the Grrab icon and pick your Clip Size — Original, 512, 1024, 1600, or 3K. Every image you clip arrives at that resolution, no matter what size the source was.

Larger images are resized down. Smaller images are enlarged with an on-device AI super-resolution model — the kind of model that reconstructs detail, not just interpolates pixels.

Nothing is uploaded. The model runs on your machine. See Privacy and data for more.

Your clip history

Sign in with Google, and every clip is saved to your history — an organized collection you can see in the side panel.

Each entry keeps the source page, timestamp, and thumbnail. Your history is accessible from any device you sign in on, and clips stay until you delete them.

Clipping to the clipboard works without signing in. Signing in adds the history.

To browse, re-copy, or export your clips, see The side panel.

The side panel

The side panel is where your clip history lives. Open it by clicking “Open SidePanel” in the Grrab popup.

Every clip appears as a card with its thumbnail, source, and timestamp, grouped by date (Today, Yesterday, and so on). Cards are labeled by source — Pinterest, YouTube, Instagram, and more. From here you can:

  • Re-copy any clip to your clipboard for another paste
  • Upload selected clips to your chosen destination, in your chosen file format (Original, JPG, JPEG, PNG, or WebP)
  • Delete clips you no longer need

Your upload destination is set at the top of the side panel. Grrab defaults to your Downloads folder, and you can switch to a local folder of your choice or your Google Drive (into a folder called “Grrab_files”) anytime. You can also change the file format next to the destination button.

Once your destination is set, select clips and click Upload — Grrab sends them straight there.

Under the hood

Privacy and data

Grrab is designed to keep your data — and your workflow — your own.

AI upscaling runs entirely on your device: the model is bundled with the extension and executes in your browser, so no image is ever uploaded for upscaling.

When you clip without signing in, nothing about the image, the page, or you leaves your browser. Grrab sends one empty request that counts that a clip happened — no URL, no page, no image, no account or device identifier — so we can see overall usage. It can be turned off.

When you sign in, your clip history is stored on Grrab’s own backend under your account — never shared with third parties.

Grrab doesn’t use third-party analytics or trackers, doesn’t monitor your browsing history, and only acts on images you interact with. For the full breakdown, see the Privacy Policy.
